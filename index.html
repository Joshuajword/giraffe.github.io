THE PHENOMENON KNOWN AS GIRAFFE

Webster's defines the giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) as a large fleet African ruminant mammal that is the tallest of living quadrapeds and has a very long nexk and a shirt coat with dark blotches separated by pale lines. I like to think that those of us who know and have studied giraffes know they are much more than a simple dictionary entry. They are the most unique of all of the animals, and one of the closest things to a dinosuar we have left...

Giraffes can be as tall as 18 feet, or more. The tallest known giraffe alive today is named forest. Adult bull giraffes have the longest necks at around 8 feet, which is a pretty long neck, all things considered.
